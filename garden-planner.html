Welcome
Your garden planning helper

This app helps you decide what to plant in your raised bed — and where to put each crop so everything gets enough sun, doesn't crowd its neighbours, and fits the time of year.

You tell it about your garden on the left. You design your bed in the middle. It gives you feedback on the right. This guide walks you through it step by step — takes about two minutes to read.

Step 1 of 6
Tell it about your garden

Find the Site tab on the left. Drag the slider to match how many hours of sun your garden gets on a typical day. Then choose which side of the bed is closest to a wall or fence (it casts shade), and set the current season.

If you have a trellis — a wire or wooden frame your climbing plants can grow up — flip the toggle on. This lets the app suggest cucumbers and beans that grow upward instead of sprawling.

Step 2 of 6
Pick what you want to grow

Click the Crops tab on the left. You'll see a list of 20 vegetables and herbs. Click ✓ Fits my site at the top to hide crops that won't do well in your garden's conditions — the ones that remain are sorted by how well they'll grow for you, best first.

Click any crop name to select it — it gets highlighted with a border. Now you're ready to place it in your bed.

Step 3 of 6
Paint crops into your bed

The big grid in the middle is your 4×4 ft garden bed — each square is one square foot. With a crop selected in the Crops tab, just click any square to plant it there. Click more squares to fill in that crop. Then go back to the Crops list, pick something else, and keep going.

Made a mistake? Click ✕ Erase in the toolbar and click a square to clear it, or press ↩ Undo to step back.

Step 4 of 6
Read your score

The Score tab on the right shows a number out of 100 — that's your whole bed's quality score. Below it, each crop gets its own bar showing how well it fits the spots you've placed it.

Green means that crop is in a great spot. Yellow means it'll grow but isn't ideal. Orange means something is off — maybe not enough sun, or it needs a trellis it doesn't have. The goal is to get as many bars green as you can.

⚠Compact Tomato needs 7+ hrs of sun — your bed only gets 5 hrs. It may not ripen well.
✓Beans next to lettuce is a classic combo — beans put nitrogen back into the soil that leafy crops love.
ℹLettuce and arugula can be sown every 2–3 weeks for a continuous harvest all season.
Step 5 of 6
Check the Notes for advice

Click the Notes tab on the right to see personalised feedback about your layout. There are three types:

🟠 Amber warnings flag real problems — a crop that won't get enough sun, or a plant that needs a climbing frame you haven't set up.
🟢 Green notes celebrate things going well — good companion plantings or ideal conditions.
🔵 Blue tips suggest improvements or handy techniques like planting in batches for a longer harvest.

Step 6 of 6
Let the app suggest a layout — or save yours

Not sure where to start? Click ✦ Auto-fill in the toolbar — the app will fill your entire bed with a smart layout based on your site conditions and goal. You can then switch to ✏ Manual mode and tweak any cell you like.

Your layout saves automatically every time you make a change — you'll see "Saved just now" appear in the toolbar. Need to plan multiple beds? Use the Bed 1 / 2 / 3 / 4 buttons at the top of the left panel to switch between separate layouts.
